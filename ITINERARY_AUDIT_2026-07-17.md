# Fall 2026 itinerary audit

Audited July 17, 2026. This is the evidence log behind the public trip page; volatile rules, schedules, prices and operating hours still need final checks.

## Verdict

**Keep Hong Kong → Chongqing → Shanghai → Taipei, November 12–29. Do not add a fifth hotel base.** This is the best total trip: four genuinely different cities, no stay under three nights, nonstop regional flights, a coherent 240-hour mainland transit, Thanksgiving in Taipei and no Chinese public-holiday collision.

Two cautions change confidence, not the route:

1. **Shenzhen is expendable.** The five-day Shenzhen SEZ port visa officially exists, but current approval for a U.S. passport is not guaranteed and 2026 traveler reports conflict. The trip must be complete without it.
2. **The return is tiring.** Sunday, November 29 normally reaches Austin that same calendar day, but leaves no real recovery day before Monday. Return November 30 or keep Monday free if possible.

## Ten complete audit passes

### 1. Dates, weekdays and recovery

All weekdays and nights were independently recomputed. Five Hong Kong nights absorb arrival fatigue, three Chongqing nights yield two full days, five Shanghai nights allow a day trip or slow day, and four Taipei nights preserve Thanksgiving. **Pass**, with the post-trip Monday flagged.

### 2. Flights, airports and buffers

Current schedule databases show nonstop depth on every regional leg: HKG–CKG is about 2h50 with several carriers, CKG–SHA has roughly ten daily nonstops, and SHA–TSA has several daily nonstops plus PVG–TPE alternatives. AUS–HKG and TPE–AUS need a connection. Use a protected open-jaw long-haul ticket, nonstop regional one-ways, about three hours at international/cross-strait airports and two hours at CKG for the domestic flight. Never book HKG–CKG through another mainland airport. **Pass**; exact November inventory and fares remain live booking checks.

### 3. Entry law and border sequence

China's National Immigration Administration explicitly allows eligible U.S. nationals to enter at a designated 240-hour port, travel across permitted areas in participating provinces/municipalities and leave from another designated port for a third country or region. The official Chinese explanation specifically permits cross-provincial travel.

The sequence **Hong Kong → Chongqing/Shanghai → Taiwan** qualifies structurally. Entering CKG November 17 starts the ordinary clock at 00:00 November 18; it ends 23:59 November 27. The November 25 Shanghai exit leaves about two days. Carry the confirmed Shanghai–Taipei ticket, hotels and full route on paper. Final admission remains a border decision.

A Shenzhen outing is a separate admission that ends at mainland exit control on return to Hong Kong; it neither starts nor extends the later 240-hour clock. Never fly Shenzhen–Chongqing on the Shenzhen-only visa. **Main transit passes strongly; Shenzhen stays high-variance.**

### 4. Geography, pacing and physical load

After one westward flight to Chongqing, the route moves east without backtracking. Each transfer is one flight and each arrival evening is light. Chongqing is the physically hardest stop; flatter Shanghai follows it. Dragon's Back, Chongqing stairs and Elephant Mountain are options, not three mandatory workouts. **Pass.**

### 5. Attractions, closures, weather and daylight

No known closure conflict breaks the plan. Shanghai Museum East is currently closed Tuesdays; the visit is Sunday. November is warm/drier in Hong Kong, mild but gray/damp in Chongqing, cool with possible rain in Shanghai, and mild with northeast-monsoon showers in Taipei. Every city has indoor fallbacks. Do not lock Jiufen, hikes or skyline decks until visibility is known; recheck all 2026 hours 15 days out. **Pass**, with Chongqing visibility and Taipei rain as forecast risks.

### 6. Fun, food, hotels and group fit

The mix is unusually balanced: harbor/hiking/Cantonese Hong Kong; vertical-city spectacle and hotpot in Chongqing; architecture, museums and polished dining in Shanghai; family, night markets and decompression in Taipei. Current checks support the named anchor restaurants as operating, but no meal is route-critical. Keep hotels refundable until room configurations and traveler count are final. Displayed prices are planning bands, not quotes. **Pass.**

### 8. Cost and value

The protected open jaw is the biggest price variable. Regional flights are sensible because rail would consume too much time on this geography. Five Shanghai nights create an inexpensive Suzhou rail option. Four bases are the practical ceiling. Reprice the whole party including bags, seats, refundable terms and room occupancy. **Good structure; exact total not yet verifiable.**

### 9. Contradictions and failure modes

The main source conflict is Shenzhen VOA issuance to U.S. citizens. Official material confirms the visa but not a current U.S.-passport guarantee or dependable hours; 2026 travelers report approvals and refusals. Therefore it cannot be presented as dependable. Other failures have easy pivots: stay in Hong Kong, swap a hike for a museum/spa, skip a view in bad weather, and rebook the Shanghai exit before mainland entry if its schedule changes. **Pass after downgrading Shenzhen confidence.**

### 10. Adversarial final challenge

The route was challenged as too long, too indirect and too border-heavy. Seventeen nights justify the trans-Pacific travel, Chongqing supplies the sharpest contrast, and Taipei is independently justified by Thanksgiving while providing the gentlest final stop. It fails only if Shenzhen is treated as guaranteed, transfer days are overloaded or everyone expects a normal Monday after returning. None is required. **Final result: keep route and dates.**
