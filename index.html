The final shape

Hong Kong→Chongqing→Shanghai→Taipei

Booking rule

Protect the chain

At Chongqing immigration, carry the confirmed Shanghai → Taipei ticket. If a schedule change moves that flight beyond the 240-hour window, fix it before departure.

Printed backup passport · hotels · onward ticket
Do not accidentally book this: Hong Kong → Chongqing with a connection at another mainland airport. Mainland immigration happens at the first mainland stop, changing the entry port and clock paperwork.

What is actually worth doing

Re-ranked July 2026: official guides establish what is open; current travel blogs explain how visits actually work; TripAdvisor review patterns and recent traveler forums expose crowd traps. “Essential” means protect the time. “If clear” and “quick hit” mean do not force it.

Nov 12–17 · 5 nights

Hong Kong

The landing pad and the food capital: harbour, hills, dense neighborhoods and enough time to absorb the flight.

Traveler-backed priorities

EssentialThe Peak + Lugard RoadTake bus 15 up if the tram queue is ugly, walk the free Lugard loop through blue hour, then take the tram down. The view is the must—not waiting 90 minutes or paying for Sky Terrace.
EssentialCentral → Sheung Wan on footTai Kwun, the Mid-Levels escalator, Man Mo Temple, dried-seafood streets and a ride on the “ding ding” tram reveal more of Hong Kong than another observation deck.
EssentialStar Ferry + harbourfrontRide near sunset. Pair it with M+ if the group wants art; otherwise use West Kowloon’s waterfront and the Tsim Sha Tsui promenade without turning Avenue of Stars into a standalone event.
If dryDragon’s BackThe 8 km ridge-to-beach hike is repeatedly recommended because it shows the green, coastal city. Replace it with M+, Tai Kwun or neighborhood wandering in rain or low cloud.

Eat with intent

  • One anchor: The Chairman if the reservation drop works; otherwise Yardbird or Ho Lee Fook.
  • Roast goose: Yat Lok in Central; order a quarter goose with lai fun.
  • Fast classics: Mak’s noodles, Kau Kee curry beef brisket, Australia Dairy Company breakfast.
  • Night: Temple Street or a dai pai dong; do not turn every dinner into a tasting menu.
Do not burn time
Avenue of Stars is a pleasant connector, not a half-day attraction. Skip the Observation Wheel and Sky100 unless the weather is perfect and someone specifically wants another paid skyline view.

Conditional bonus · no hotel

Shenzhen in one day

If the VOA is issued, give the day one clear theme: see the young megacity’s hardware markets, transformation story and skyline. The train is short; the border process is the variable.

Target: Saturday, Nov 14. Reconfirm VOA-office hours the week of travel.

Cross at Luohu or Huanggang. West Kowloon does not issue the Shenzhen VOA. Bring passport photos if possible, RMB/payment backup, Hong Kong hotel details and onward flights.
Start at Huaqiangbei. The electronics ecosystem is more distinctively Shenzhen than a replica theme park. Browse SEG Plaza and adjacent markets without planning a major purchase.
Understand the city, then see it. Choose Shenzhen Museum’s reform-era galleries, then Lianhua Hill for the Civic Center skyline. Add Ping An’s paid deck only in exceptional visibility.
Return to Hong Kong that evening. Mainland exit inspection formally closes this entry; keep the VOA page, transport receipts and Hong Kong landing slip.
Do not burn time
Skip Window of the World, Splendid China, distant Shekou and a cross-city mall crawl. Reviews and one-day guides consistently favor a compact Futian/Huaqiangbei experience over zigzagging to Nanshan.

Nov 17–20 · 3 nights

Chongqing

Three dimensions, two rivers and one intentionally spicy dinner. The city itself is the attraction.

Traveler-backed priorities

EssentialRide Line 2 through LizibaThe experience is riding the elevated railway through the building and along the river. Give the packed street viewpoint 15 minutes, then continue to Fotuguan/Eling instead of waiting for the same photo.
EssentialShancheng Lane + ShibatiStairs, rooftops, cliffside alleys and surprise level changes are the real “8D city.” Start high, walk down and let this replace a checklist of viral viewpoints.
EssentialHongya Cave—outside onlyArrive before the 19:30 lighting, photograph it from Qiansimen Bridge or the opposite bank, then move on. Review consensus is clear: the glowing exterior is special; the retail interior is not.
If queue is shortYangtze Cableway + riversRide only with a timed ticket or a wait under roughly 45 minutes. Otherwise take a ferry or walk the new riverside path to Chaotianmen for the same geographic payoff.

Eat with intent

  • Hotpot: Peijie for a central first-timer option; Pipayuan only if the massive hillside spectacle is worth the drive.
  • Order: 微辣 (mild), split pot if offered, tripe, duck blood, goose intestine, tofu skin and cilantro meatballs.
  • Breakfast: Chongqing xiaomian; follow office-worker queues rather than a viral map pin.
  • Wet-weather anchor: Three Gorges Museum gives historical context when the skyline disappears.
Do not burn time
Skip Hongya Cave’s interior, Raffles City’s generic mall levels and far-off Pipayuan unless spectacle matters more than food. Ciqikou is optional on this short stay: atmospheric early, shoulder-to-shoulder retail later.

Nov 20–25 · 5 nights

Shanghai

The longest mainland stay earns a rhythm: old lanes, world-class museums, skyline theatre and one slow day.

Traveler-backed priorities

EssentialThe Bund at blue hourWalk north toward Waibaidu Bridge while the historic façades and Pudong skyline light up. The free riverfront delivers the iconic view; return by public ferry rather than the sightseeing tunnel.
EssentialFormer French Concession slowlyWukang Mansion is one photo, not the event. Continue along Wukang, Anfu and Fuxing roads for plane trees, villas, cafés, boutiques and neighborhood texture.
EssentialShanghai Museum EastProtect a focused half-day for the strongest galleries and the scheduled Met jewelry exhibition. Reserve if required; do not combine it with three other museums.
Go at openingYu Garden—not just the bazaarThe actual Ming garden earns 90 minutes before crowds. The surrounding City God Temple bazaar is photogenic but heavily commercial; leave when it stops being fun.
Choose oneSuzhou or West BundSuzhou’s classical gardens are the stronger full-day change of scene. West Bund is the better low-logistics choice in cold rain. Zhujiajiao is the compromise, not the automatic must.

Eat with intent

  • Local splurge: Fu 1088 in its 1920s townhouse; reserve a group room.
  • XLB: Jia Jia Tang Bao at 127 Huanghe Road and Lai Lai Xiao Long at 506 Tianjin Road.
  • Shanghainese comfort: Ren He Guan or Old Jesse for red-braised pork, smoked fish and seasonal crab.
  • View meal: Mr & Mrs Bund if the group wants one international dinner with the skyline.
Do not burn time
Use East Nanjing Road only as a connector unless shopping is the goal. Skip the Bund sightseeing tunnel and do not stack Shanghai Tower, Oriental Pearl and rooftop drinks—the skyline repeats.

Nov 25–29 · 4 nights

Taipei

A soft landing after mainland China: family, night markets, hot springs and the best transit of the trip.

Traveler-backed priorities

EssentialLongshan → Bopiliao → DihuaTemple ritual, preserved streets, tea, fabric shops and everyday old-city life make a coherent Thanksgiving morning. Dihua is better wandered than treated as a shopping checklist.
EssentialNational Palace Museum—focusedIts 8,900-review consensus supports the trip across town, but not gallery exhaustion. Choose two or three collections, allow roughly three hours and expect large tour groups.
If visibility is goodElephant Mountain + XinyiClimb before sunset for the best 101 composition. The observatory is optional afterward; choose it for the tuned-mass damper and tower experience, not because the outdoor view was insufficient.
Forecast decidesBeitou or JiufenBeitou is the reliable recovery choice in drizzle. Jiufen is more atmospheric but should be visited early or late; pair it with one hike or Shifen waterfall, not a rushed Yehliu–Shifen–Jiufen bus marathon.

Eat with intent

  • Thanksgiving: Nov 26 with Jen’s family—the fixed emotional anchor of the Taipei stay.
  • Night market: Raohe for Fuzhou pepper buns, herbal pork-rib soup and oyster vermicelli.
  • Taiwanese splurge: Mountain and Sea House for refined heritage cooking; reserve.
  • Everyday classics: Din Tai Fung, Jin Feng braised pork rice, beef noodle soup and soy milk breakfast.
Do not burn time
Skip the three-stop northeast-coast package on this short stay. Shilin is the biggest night market, not automatically the best; Raohe or Ningxia is easier to graze. Ximending is optional unless the group wants youth retail and neon.
